# Document Guide

In their original form, the documents are untitled, and they are not numbered or dated. In the list below, the title is used as it appears in the first line of the document[^1].

## A: Primary Preparatory Works, the Chiha drafts (DOC 1 to 15)

Section A includes 15 documents where the imprint of Chiha is certain. They consist of manuscripts as well as texts typed with annotations in Michel Chiha’s handwriting, texts typed with annotations which are not in Chiha’s handwriting, and texts printed with or without annotations. All are in French except DOC 14 and 15 which are respectively the Arabic printed constitutional project at its latest stage before distribution to the Council of Representatives for plenary discussion and adoption.

### A1 (DOCs 1 to 4)

Includes successive versions based on Chiha’s first manuscript. Consist of 85 articles, starting with the first manuscript version in Chiha’s handwriting. DOC 1 is then typed, and insertions and comments are added by hand to the subsequent versions.

[DOC 1](/works/doc-01-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-manusc.html) – _Titre 1er: Dispositions fondamentales_ (manuscript, in Michel Chiha’s handwriting), 85 arts., 15 pp.

[DOC 2](/works/doc-02-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) – _Titre 1er: Dispositions fondamentales_ (typed version of DOC 1, with annotations not in Chiha’s handwriting), 85 arts., 25 pp.

[DOC 3](/works/doc-03-avant-projet-de-constitution-pour-le-grand.html) – _Avant-projet de Constitution pour le Grand Liban_ (typed version of DOC 1, with some annotations not in Chiha’s handwriting), 85 arts., 27 pp.

[DOC 4](/works/doc-04-constitution-du-grand-liban-typed-version-o.html) – _Constitution du Grand Liban_ (typed version of DOC 1, with annotations in Chiha’s handwriting), 85 arts., 25 pp.

### A2 (DOCs 5 to 10)

This set provides an overview of the modifications made to DOCs 1 to 4. The versions are typewritten and corrected by Chiha. From DOC 5 onwards, they include various annotations and amendments by Chiha, Henri de Jouvenel, as well as by other contributors whose handwriting could not be identified. The sequence of the documents appears relatively clear in light of the type of medium (manuscript or typewritten), the linguistic and substantive developments, and the respective interventions of Chiha and the other annotators.

DOCs 5 to 8 comprise 96 articles and provide a complete text covering the internal organisation of Lebanon within the Constitution. These documents were subsequently consolidated in DOC 11, to which a section concerning the subordination of Lebanon to the Mandate was added.

[DOC 5](/works/doc-05-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) – _Titre Ier, Dispositions fondamentales_ (typewritten version of DOC 1, annotated by Chiha), 96 articles, 25 pages.

[DOC 6](/works/doc-06-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) – _Titre Ier, Dispositions fondamentales_ (typewritten version of DOC 5, annotated by Chiha and Henri de Jouvenel), 96 articles, 14 pages.

[DOC 7](/works/doc-07-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) – _Title I, Fundamental Provisions_ (typewritten version of DOC 5, containing two sets of handwritten annotations, one of which is by Chiha), 96 articles, 14 pages.

[DOC 8](/works/doc-08-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) – _Title I, Fundamental Provisions_ (typewritten version of DOC 5, annotated by Chiha), 96 articles, 14 pages.

[DOC 9](/works/doc-09-souchier-observations-observations-sur-le-p.html) – _Observations on the draft Constitution_ (typewritten), with comments (referred to by Mallat as the “Souchier Observations”).

The main changes between the first set (DOCs 1 to 4) and the second lie notably in the addition of Article 5 concerning the flag, leading to a handwritten renumbering of the provisions. DOC 5 also includes occasional insertions: a half handwritten page added (serving as page 7) with Articles 27 and 28; Article 33 added on p. 8; Article 35 on p. 9; Article 43 on p. 12; Articles 52, 53 and 54 on p. 14; Article 59 on p. 16; Article 65 on p. 17; Article 89 on p. 24; as well as Articles 95 and 97 on p. 25.

### A3 (DOCs 11 to 15)

are final versions. This set of documents is close to each other and appears as the late versions which circulated in Paris and Beirut before submission to the plenary discussion in the Council of Representatives on 19 May. DOCs 11 to 13 were probably circulated in late April to mid-May 1926, just before the final debates in the Council of Representatives.

[DOC 11](/works/doc-11-titre-1er-dispositions-fondamentales-typed.html) is a clean, typed version of DOC 8, which consolidates all the previous versions and includes—set in a different typeface—the section on the French Mandate, comprising ten articles added after Article 96. The document represents the most recent available iteration of the “Chiha Constitution”. By and large, it was adopted as the Constitution.

[DOC 15](/works/doc-15-ar-al-dustur-al-lubnani-lebanese-constituti.html) includes the amendments introduced by the Council of Representatives and adopted as the Constitution on 23 May at the close of the plenary debate. It is arguably the first published version of the Constitution (Mallat p. 99).

## B: Preparatory works (DOCs 16 to 25)

The documents in this section B are, according to Mallat, less important preparatory works, but they provide material worth investigating further for the options concerning executive power and the presidency. They consist of incomplete notes, studies and partial drafts either by Chiha or considered by him, and full constitutional drafts sent to him for inspiration by other authors.

This section is divided into six parts:

### B1. Contemporaneous constitutions (DOC 16)

[DOC 16](/works/doc-16-file-entitled-constitutions-diverses-all-pr.html) presents the covers of printed pamphlets of several constitutions preserved in the Chiha archives. They include, in particular, texts in French relating to Latvia, Lithuania, the League of Nations and the Mandates, the constitutional system of the Egyptian State, Finland, Romania, the Anglo-Iraqi Treaty signed in Baghdad, as well as the French Mandate for Syria and Lebanon. Dating from the early 1920s, these documents, which are no longer in force today, contributed to the development of Chiha’s thinking. The complete texts now accessible online, are not reproduced on this platform.

### B2. File covers (DOC 17–18)

DOCs 17 and 18 consist of a few words written by Chiha on a cover page for a file. Because of reshuffles of the documents carried out over time, it is not possible to ascertain which documents were included in these files.

[DOC 17](/works/doc-17-conseil-representatif-projets-en-cours-hand.html)- _Conseil Représentatif, Projets en cours_ (handwritten by Chiha, one page).
This is a file cover with no content other than the title. It is included because it appears to refer to texts by members of the Council of Representatives and/or documents collected by or for them..

[DOC 18](/works/doc-18-dernier-texte-dernieres-notes-last-text-las.html)- _Dernier Texte, dernières notes_ (‘Last text — last notes’, handwritten by Chiha, one page).
This is also a simple file cover. The missing document(s) inside would have been the latest version which Chiha worked on.

### B3. Partial drafts probably prepared by or with Chiha (DOC 19–21)

[DOC 19](/works/doc-19-chiv-du-pouvoir-executif-in-mcs-handwriting.html)- _Ch. IV Du Pouvoir Exécutif_ (handwritten by Chiha), 25 Arts., 6 pp. (Arts. 50 to 75)
The document reads like a preliminary study for DOC 1. This substantially revised document is presented as a preliminary study of DOC 1. It contains gaps (missing pages and discontinuities in the numbering). Its most significant section consists of a list of themes to be developed (constitutional revision, Congress, the judiciary, the High Court, public finances, and transitional provisions).

[DOC 20](/works/doc-20-projet-de-constitution-libanaise-typed-also.html)- _Projet de Constitution Libanaise_ (typed, also on executive power). 19 Arts., 3 p.
[DOC 21](/works/doc-21-projet-de-constitution-libanaise-typed-also.html)- _Projet de Constitution Libanaise_ (typed, also on executive power). 29 Arts., 4 p.

These two documents may be interpreted as preparatory drafts for the section relating to the executive authority in DOC 1.
Subsections B4 to B6 also provide useful material, particularly with regard to executive power and the presidency.

### B4. Menassa’s letter and preliminary constitutional draft (DOC 22)

1. [DOC 22](/works/doc-22-monsieur-le-depute-typed-avant-projet-de-co.html) – Letter and preliminary draft of the Lebanese Constitution (typewritten), 110 articles, 10 pages.

This document consists of a letter from Gabriel Menassa to Michel Chiha, dated 28 December 1925 (Paris), accompanied by a complete constitutional draft. It is likely that it was received prior to the drafting of DOC 1.

### B5. The ‘Chiha/Trad Drafts’ (DOCs 23–24)

[DOC 23](/works/doc-23-constitution-de-letat-du-grand-liban-typed.html)- _Constitution de l’Etat du Grand-Liban_ (typed with annotations not in Chiha’s handwriting). 110 Arts., 19 pp.
[DOC 24](/works/doc-24-constitution-de-letat-du-grand-liban-typed.html)- _Constitution de l’État du Grand-Liban_ (typed, no annotations), 110 Arts., 18 pp.

The position of these documents within the sequence remains uncertain. Mallat suggests that they may precede DOC 5 and result from a combination of Chiha’s drafts (Docs 1 to 4) and a proposal by Petro Trad (a lawyer and member of the Council of Representatives in 1926, mentioned in various sources alongside Chiha as an “author” of the Constitution). This hypothesis, however, remains speculative.

### B6. Another constitutional draft, author unknown (DOC 25)

[DOC 25](/works/doc-25-titre-1er-disposition-generales-typed-with.html)- _Titre 1er Disposition Générales_ (typed, with typed edits), 54 Arts., 21 pp.

This document comprises 54 articles, the last two of which deal with Lebanon’s international relations and its position vis-à-vis France and the League of Nations (SDN). A few of the opening articles are closest to those found in the earliest versions drafted by Chiha, but the remainder diverges from them quite significantly. Nothing in the document makes it possible to identify its author or to date it precisely. It is assumed, however, that it was completed around the time when Documents 1 to 4 were being prepared.

An alternative reading of Trad’s contribution in Documents 23 and 24, together with the distinctive nature of the draft of Document 25 and the fact that it is typewritten with a wide right-hand margin allowing space for annotations, suggests that it may have been drafted by a Francophile member of the Council of Representatives (Mallat).

## C: Miscellaneous (Doc 26 to 29)

This part includes four documents in Arabic, with limited relevance to the elaboration of the Constitution. DOCs 26 and 27 are constitutional drafts by jurist politicians (Ayyub Tabet, Sami al-Solh) who sent them to Chiha, probably too late, however, to provide any significant input to the drafting process. Tabet (1884-1947) was a prominent figure during the Mandate Period and was briefly President just before Independence. Solh (c. 1900-1968) was a jurist and Beirut politician who became President of the Council of Ministers several times from 1942 to his death.

[DOC 26](/works/doc-26-ar-dr-ayyub-tabet-mashru-al-nazam-al-asasi.html)- In Arabic. Dr Ayyub Tabet, _Mashru‘ al-nazam al-asasi li-dawlat Lubnan_ (project of a basic law for the State of Lebanon), Beirut: Matba‘at al-misbah 1926 (printed booklet). 74 Arts., 17 pp.
It is a printed booklet in Arabic with a dedication by Dr Ayyub Tabet to Chiha dated 25 March 1926.

[DOC 27](/works/doc-27-ar-projet-de-sami-bey-el-solh-french-handwr.html)- In Arabic. _Projet de Sami Bey El Solh_ (handwritten words in French not by Chiha, under heading Al-jumhuriyya al-lubnaniyya (Lebanese Republic official heading), (typed) 1 Art., 4 pp.
It was sent to Chiha by ‘Anis Saleh, Directeur Général de la Justice’ on an attached business card. Anis Saleh (d. 1973) was then the top administrative official in the Ministry of Justice.

[DOC 28](/works/doc-28-ar-mashru-qanun-draft-electoral-law-typed-w.html)- In Arabic. _mashru‘ qanun_ (Draft \[electoral\] law) (typed with annotations by Chiha on top of page 1) (n.d.). 61 Arts., 11 pp.
DOC 28 includes a reference in Art. 61 to ‘the abrogation of Decision 2 of 2 January 1924’. It constitutes a draft electoral law of relevance to the 1925 Electoral Law, but not to the Constitution of 1926.

[DOC 29](/works/doc-29-ar-bayn-yadayy-al-majlis-in-the-hands-of-th.html)- In Arabic.‘\[_bayn\] yadayy al-majlis’_ (‘in the hands of the council’). Typed, 4 p.
DOC 29 mentions the Constitution of 1926, so it was written after its adoption. The document refers to constitutional amendments enacted in 1927. It is therefore not relevant to the elaboration of the initial text. There is no title in the document and ‘in the hands of the council’ are the first words in the text, which starts at page two. Page one is missing. The text reads like a translated list of suggested amendments, by Chiha or possibly by the French authorities.

According to Mallat, these documents were submitted too late to have any meaningful influence on the drafting of the constitutional text.

[^1]: Sequence of documents suggested by Ch Mallat in _Democracy Redefined: Michel Chiha and the Lebanese Constitution, Edinburgh_ University Press, 2025.
