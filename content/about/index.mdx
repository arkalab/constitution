<SectionTitle>Michel Chiha's Constitutional Papers</SectionTitle>

<SectionBody>

Welcome to the digital archive of Michel Chiha’s Constitutional Papers—a collection of
exceptional historical and intellectual significance for understanding the foundations of
modern Lebanon. Preserved within the Michel Chiha family archives since his passing in
1954, these documents are now made available to the public on the centenary of the 1926
Lebanese Constitution.

</SectionBody>

<SectionCTA href="/search">View Papers</SectionCTA>

<SectionBody>

Produced between December 1925 and May 1926, the papers document the debates,
reflections, and drafting processes that shaped the Lebanese Constitution, later adopted by the
Council of Representatives. They offer a rare and direct insight into the ideas, tensions, and
aspirations that informed the creation of the Lebanese state, capturing a pivotal moment in its
political and legal history.

Most of the original documents are untitled, unnumbered, and undated. To facilitate
navigation while preserving their integrity, each piece has been identified by its opening line
and assigned a reference code beginning with “DOC-1.” This approach enables readers to
engage with the material in a structured way while following the development of
constitutional thought as it unfolded.

The organization of the archive follows the structure established by constitutional scholar
Prof. Chibli Mallat in his recent study of these papers. Adopting his analytical framework
provides both clarity and scholarly continuity, offering users a guided yet open exploration of
the archive.

This archive aims to serve as both a scholarly resource and a civic space for revisiting the
principles that continue to define Lebanon’s constitutional identity.

</SectionBody>
